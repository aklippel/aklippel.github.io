My Top 5 Albums of 2020

#1 Sawayama by Rina Sawayama

Sawayama is the debut album of Rina Sawayama.  Sawayama offers an eclectic range of pop songs, exploring themes such as identity, belonging, and consumerism. While some critics have criticized the album’s lack of unity, I find that the tension between each track’s pop subgenera contributes the Sawayama’s overall story of feeling trapped between her Japanese and British identities.

My top three tracks include Akasaka Sad, Who's Gonna Save U Now?, and Comme Des Garçons (Like The Boys).

#2 color theory by Soccer Mommy

Sophie Allison, known by her stage name, Soccer Mommy, released her second studio album, color theory in Febuary of 2020. color theory offers a mid-tempo, 2000s pop inspired, indie rock album that leads its listeners through the colored phases of Sophie Allison's own emotional introspection. Dropping just a few short weeks before the COVID-19 pandemic forced myself and much of the country into a lockdown, color theory struck a well-timed chord with me. circle the drain was an early favorite of mine off the album.

Other top tracks include lucy and up the walls.

#3 Punisher by Phoebe Bridgers

Punisher is the second solo album of Phoebe Bridgers.  After two years of collaborative albums, (boygenius & Better Oblivion Community Center), this solo project was highly anticipated by fans of Bridger's debut, Stranger in the Alps (2017). With tracks like Savior Complex and Punisher, Bridgers delivers haunting tracks that have become her signiture. Kyoto presents a more upbeat option, but hits with the bittersweetness of Bridger's strained relationship with her father.

My top tracks include Savior Complex, Kyoto, and Graceland Too.

#4 Jump Rope Gazers by The Beths

New Zealand rock group The Beth's sophmore album, Jump Rope Gazers, was released in 2020. After the title track offtheir debut album, Future Me Hates Me, earned the title of my most played track of 2019, I knew I would have to give Jump Rope Gazers a spin. In their previous body of work, The Beth's greatest strength has been their cheeky, high-tempo, rock anthems. This album delivered this with Dying to Believe, while treading into new territory with the much calmer Do You Want Me Now? and You Are a Beam of Light. T he Beths' sound has always offered me a strange nostalgia. Despite not quite living up to their debut album, Jump Rope Gazers stays consistent with delivering to that sound, despite slowing down their songs.

My top tracks include Dying to Believe, Do You Want Me Now?, and Don't Go Away.

#5 Moddern Jonny Sings: Songs in the Age of Vibe by Theo Katzman

Modern Johnny Sings: Songs in the Age of Vibe is Theo Katzman's third studio album. However, most of his musical success has come from performing with Vulfpeck, a jazz-funk rhythm section Katzman joined while a music student at the University of Michigan. Modern Johnny offers some of the silly, and in this case slightly political, songs Vulfpeck has become known for, with You Could Be President and (I Don't Want to Be A) Billionaire. However, where Katzman's songwriting strengths lie is with his "Heartbreak Hits," a phrase he used as the title for his second album.

For me, the runaway track is What Did You Mean (When You Said Love). Simple and fairly stripped, this track pulls heavy emotional punches with its lyrics. My other top tracks include The Death of Us and Hardly Ever Rains.
